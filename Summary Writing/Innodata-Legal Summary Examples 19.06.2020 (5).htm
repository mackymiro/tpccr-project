Example 1: New legislation entry

Romania[CJ1] 

EN: Law No. 59/2016 on the control of major-accident hazards involving dangerous substances[CJ2] 

RO: Lege nr. 59 din 11 aprilie 2016 privind controlul asupra pericolelor de accident major n care sunt implicate substanțe periculoase[CJ3] 

 

Link to full text >> Law no. 59[CJ4] 

 

Purpose & Scope

This Law regulates measures to prevent major accidents involving dangerous substances as well as to limit their consequences for human health and the environment, in order to ensure a high level of protection in a consistent and effective manner. These sites are typically referred to as "Seveso" sites in reference to the place that instigated such legislation at EU level.

It sets out requirements for operators that have the capacity to hold significant amounts of dangerous substances. This are defined into two groups which determine the level of control required:

         lower tier sites

         upper tier sites

Notification

Where an operator considers that a dangerous substance or substances falling within the scope of the provisions set out in Annex I is present or could be present on site, in excess of the thresholds given, they must notify the competent authorities with a justification of the measures in place to prevent a major accident beyond the site.

Assessment

For the purposes of an assessment of the possibility of a major accident the operator must provide the following information to the National Agency for Environmental Protection:

         detailed inventory;

         physical and chemical properties;

         potential hazards (reactivity, flammability, toxicity, etc);

         environmental risks;

         class of the substance according to the Regulation (EC) 1272/2008;

         information on operation conditions.

General obligations

         take all measures to prevent major accidents;

         to prove to authorities that all such measures have been taken;

         appoint site security manager;

         submit notification to the authorities with the relevant information including safety data sheets, quantity of substances, description of the activities to be carried out and the factors that may cause major accident;

         develop Major Accident Prevention Policy and submit it to the authorities.

An operator of a higher-level site is required to prepare and submit a security report. This report aims to demonstrate that major hazards are identified, and an emergency plan is developed.

If case of any changes that may result in significant consequences in the event of major accident, the operator must notify the authorities of such changes presenting documentation reflecting such changes in safety data sheets, security report, and emergency plan.

All operators must develop and implement an emergency plan. Such plans must include measures for mitigating the effects of potential accidents.

The operator must also provide information on security measures to the general public and make a safety report and inventory of hazardous substances available for anyone to inspect.

Accident

In the event of an accident the operator must inform all competent authorities of an accident and take all necessary actions outlined in the emergency plan.

Notification Procedure of 20 December 2019 sets out that in the event of a major accident involving dangerous substance, the operator must observe the following measures:

         Inform ISUBIF (Inspectorate for Emergency Situations Bucharest-Ilfov)/ISUJ (county inspectorates for emergency situations);

         Alert the population within the impact area of a major accident

Where there may be medium- or long-term environmental effects, further information must be provided to the competent authorities within 5 working days of any intervention of immediate consequences.

After any major accident involving dangerous substances, the operator must re-examine the safety report implementing the accident policy and transmit the measures to stop its reoccurrence, to the competent authority. Measures to prevent the recurrence of major accidents must be submitted in writing and in electrical format within 90 days of completion of the intervention of the accident's consequences. Operators must have an appropriate register of any incidents/accidents.[CJ5] 

Change comment

Notification Procedure of December 20, 2020 sets out the measures to be taken in the event of a major accident involving dangerous substances.

Applicable to operators of sites holding dangerous substances. [CJ6] 

Example 2: Updated legislation entry

Ireland

Railway Safety Act 2005

SI 31 of 2005 >> link to full text,

Purpose & Scope

The Railway Safety Act 2005 establishes a comprehensive framework within which railway safety is regulated. In addition to providing for the establishment and empowerment of the Commission for Railway Regulation (CRR), and within it a functionally independent Railway Incident Investigation Unit. The principal functions of the RSC are to:

         Foster and encourage railway safety;

         Enforce this Act and any other legislation relating to railway safety; and

         Investigate and report on railway incidents.

The principal provisions of the Act relate to:

         Placement of a general duty of care on railway undertakings, persons working on the railway and others to have regard for their own safety and that of others in conducting activities on or about the railway.

         Requirement for railway undertakings to implement such systems as are necessary for the effective management of safety and to evidence these in a safety case.

         Reporting of railway incidents by undertakings and their investigations by those undertakings or by the Railway Incident Investigation Unit.

         Establishment of a Railway Safety Advisory Council representative of various stakeholder organisations and with powers to make recommendations to the Minister and the RSC on matters of railway safety.

         Control of the impact of drugs and alcohol including specification of maximum limits and testing of safety critical railway workers.

The main duties of the RSC are to:

         Assess safety cases submitted by railway operators (Iarnrod Eireann, heritage railway, the Luas operator or any other person who operates a railway) and where it is satisfied that the undertaking can operate the railway safely; issue safety certificates;

         Assess the safety of new infrastructure works and rolling stock before they are constructed, commissioned and brought into service;

         Audit the safety management system and safety case of a railway undertaking;

         Make regulations in relation to specific aspects of railway safety;

         Carry out inspections of railway infrastructure, operations and management systems and take enforcement proceedings where necessary, including the use of mandatory prohibition and improvement notices and High Court injunctions.

Amendments

SI 114/2018 provides for the levy, in accordance with section 26 of the 2005 Act, which must be paid by specified railway organisations to cover the expenditure needs of the Commission for Railway Regulation.

S.I. No. 191/2019 updates the levy, with the relevant pay information for year beginning 1st January 2019 relayed in the schedule. Heritage railways are now exempt from the levy.

Heritage railways refers to a party who exclusively operates a train service / railway infrastructure of historical or tourist interest, or whom the Railway Safety Commission has specified to be one.[CJ7] 

Change comment

S.I. No. 191/2019 documents the levy pay information schedule for 2019 and adds an exemption for heritage railways.
